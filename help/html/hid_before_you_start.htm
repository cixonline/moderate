Before You Start

Creating a New CIX Forum

Think about what (and for whom) your forum will be for. There are no limits (other than CIX's terms and conditions and the law :-) upon you.

Will your forum be technical, personal, social, spiritual, political,...? Do you want it to be openly accessible to anyone on CIX (an open forum) or do you want entry to be restricted? If so, do you want details about it to be freely available so people can ask you for entry (a closed forum) or should it's existence be revealed only to those people you choose to invite in (a closed confidential forum)?

If you are planning an open or, in most cases, a closed forum, you need to check whether there is already a forum on CIX that you would end up duplicating. Check the CIX forum list and look at the forum description for any forums that look to cover similar subjects.

You may find that an existing general forum has a topic that deals with the specific subject of your idea. Even if this is not the case, it may be more appropriate to arrange for the creation of a new topic in an existing forum (you could volunteer to co-moderate the forum) than to create an entire new forum.

Having got this far, you should post a message on CIX in the conference/suggestions topic and possibly in the noticeboard forum to see what interest and ideas you can get from other people on CIX.
